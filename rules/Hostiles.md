
# Hostiles

Hostiles are models controlled by the game (AI). They function like any other model with their own Profile Cards, and behave according to Hostile Behavior cards.

## Turn Order of Hostiles

- Hostiles perform their Active Role after all players have finished their turns.
- Hostiles regain Activation Points during every player's Upkeep Phase.
- Players regain Activation Points at the beginning of every Hostiles turn.
- Hostiles perform their Reaction Step after the Active Player's Action Step and before the Resolution Step.
- When a Hostile declares an Action targeting a model, it must rotate to face the target directly.
- If a Hostile suffers a Hit, it must turn directly towards the attacker (Exception: Hostiles with the Construct or Undead I Trait turn on Wound instead).
- Unless clearly specified otherwise, a Hostile moves via the shortest route.
- Hostiles ignore tokens unless specified otherwise.
- Hostiles can pre-measure distances even on Realism mode.

## Hostile Turn

### 1) Establish Order of Activations

Hostiles activate according to their Tiers (marked on the bottom of the AI card), beginning with the lowest Tier.

- If multiple Hostiles share the same Tier, the one with the highest Morale activates first.
- If multiple Hostiles share both the same Tier and Morale, players decide their order.
- Hostiles of the same Tier must exhaust all of their Activation Points before a higher-Tiered Hostile can activate.

### 2) Activate Hostiles

The Activation Sequence for Hostiles is the same as for regular models, however some Hostiles may have Special Behaviors, special abilities and particular Target Priorities.

### 3) Follow the Hierarchy of Behaviors

Within the numbered Hierarchy of Behaviors, the highlighted text (before the ":") states a specific condition; the non-highlighted text (after the ":") explains what the Hostile will do if the condition is met.

- Check conditions hierarchically from Behavior 1 downward until a condition is met.
- If none of the conditions are met, the Hostile does not Activate or React.
- If more than one possible target matches the condition, the model matching the Hostile's Target Priority the most becomes the target. If this is hard to discern, the Hostile moves towards the nearest enemy.
